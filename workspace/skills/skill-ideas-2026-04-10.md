# Skill ideas — 2026-04-10

## 1) openshift-pod-triage
Create a repeatable workflow for diagnosing unhealthy pods in Kubernetes/OpenShift. Cover CrashLoopBackOff, ImagePullBackOff, Pending pods, failed probes, evictions, scheduling, PVC issues, and service-account/config problems. Bundle a script that collects the most useful `oc`/`kubectl` diagnostics in one shot.

## 2) azure-devops-pr-triage
Handle Azure DevOps pull request review, comment triage, build status checks, merge blockers, and summary generation. Good fit for Patrick’s daily work, but the exact local tooling/auth path is less certain than OpenShift.

## 3) plane-crash-brief
Research an aviation incident and produce a structured briefing with timeline, aircraft, operator, sequence of events, causal factors, and lessons learned. Fun and aligned with Patrick’s interests, but less operationally valuable than work-focused skills.

## 4) homelab-change-planner
Turn rough infrastructure ideas into low-risk change plans for home servers, storage, networking, backups, and rollback. Strong long-term utility, but more planning-oriented and less deterministic.

## 5) ai-side-hustle-screen
Evaluate small AI product ideas against effort, defensibility, operational burden, pricing, and support load, then recommend what is worth building. Great strategic fit, but not as immediately actionable as the OpenShift troubleshooting skill.

## Selection
Selected: **openshift-pod-triage**

## Why this one
- High day-to-day value for Patrick’s work
- Concrete, reusable workflow
- Easy to trigger from common real requests
- Benefits from a bundled diagnostic script, not just prose
- Narrow enough to be a strong first skill instead of a vague “do DevOps” catch-all
